CREATORS & PARTNERS

GISELE BÜNDCHEN: “SIMPLICITY IS THE NEW LUXURY”

Read Time: 2 min

The supermodel’s new role as advisor to IWC’s sustainability committee.

  • ON HOW LUXURY AND FASHION CAN BE MORE SUSTAINABLE

    It’s about being mindful. I have pieces in fashion that I’ve had since I was 19 years old. Jackets, bags... If things are really well made, they can last a long time. If you look in your closet, you really wear only 5% of what you have. You’re like “Oh my god, I have nothing to wear” but you have a full closet! I have key pieces that I feel really good about, and that’s what I wear. I’m a very basic person. It’s about simplicity. Quite frankly, I believe simplicity is the new luxury. It’s about high quality but simple in the way that you don’t need so many [products].

  • ON WHICH PROJECTS AND ORGANIZATIONS GISELE CHOOSES TO WORK WITH

    The ones that have a true intention and want to make a real difference by creating the biggest impact in the social and environmental area, which are the areas that I am passionate about. “Kiss the Ground” is a documentary that I produced. It talks about the whole story of soil. Everything starts with soil. If the soil is healthy, we can be healthy because we’re consuming our food from what grows in the soil. I work with organizations like that, producing documentaries to bring awareness to how people shop and become aware what’s in their food. Because we are what we eat.

  • ON HER NEW ROLE AS ENVIRONMENTAL & COMMUNITY PROJECTS ADVISOR FOR IWC

    I feel very honored to be part of this team, because that’s how we create the biggest impact: through collaboration, right? That’s why I love that you have the Sustainability Report. Because it really shows how you’re having an impact in a practical way. To think about what we are producing, and how, in the most thoughtful and responsible way.

    I was very excited to come in and learn about everything that IWC is doing in terms of sustainability and community because one is not separate from the other. We are nature. And when nature is in equilibrium, our lives will be, too.
